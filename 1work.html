Physic

Key Notes

Core Principle
  • Energy is never created or destroyed (law of conservation).
  • Energy changes form or transfers between objects/stores.
  • Work is done when energy moves into an object’s energy store.
Types of Energy Stores
  • Heat (temperature-related)
  • Kinetic (movement)
  • Gravitational potential (position in field)
  • Elastic (stretched spring)
  • Chemical bonds
  • Magnetic
  • Electrostatic
  • Nuclear

Energy transfers between stores by: mechanically, electrically, heating, or radiation (light/sound).

Systems
  • A system is a collection of matter in physics.
  • Open system: exchanges energy and matter with outside world.
  • Closed system: no matter or energy enters/leaves.
  • Energy is conserved → zero overall change.
Kettle Example
  • Energy flows from plug socket → heating element.
  • Heating element’s thermal energy transfers to water.
  • Water’s thermal energy store increases (work done).
Work Done
  • Work can be mechanical or electrical.
  • Example: kicking a ball → chemical energy (muscles) → kinetic energy (ball).
  • When current flows, energy is needed to overcome resistance in wires.
Train Example
  • Brakes apply friction → kinetic energy (wheels) → thermal energy (surroundings).
