Electromagnetism

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) What is electromagnetism?
  • Electromagnetism is the idea that an electric current produces a magnetic field.
  • This happens around ordinary wires, coils, solenoids, and electromagnets.
  • The magnetic field only exists because current is flowing.
2) Magnetic field around a straight wire
  • A current-carrying wire creates magnetic field lines around itself.
  • The field lines form concentric circles around the wire.
  • The field is strongest closest to the wire, where the field lines are closest together.
3) The right hand rule
  • The direction of the magnetic field depends on the direction of the current.
  • Point your right thumb in the direction of the current.
  • Your curled fingers show the direction of the magnetic field.
4) Reversing the current
  • If the current reverses, the magnetic field also reverses.
  • The right hand rule can be used again to check the new direction.
  • Changing current direction changes the direction of the field lines.
5) Coils and solenoids
  • When a wire is bent into a circular coil, the magnetic fields from each side interact.
  • Adding many turns of wire makes a solenoid.
  • Inside a solenoid, the magnetic field is strong and uniform.
6) Electromagnets
  • The field outside a solenoid is similar to the field around a bar magnet.
  • Field lines come out of the north pole and enter the south pole.
  • A solenoid with current acts as an electromagnet.
7) Switching and reversing electromagnets
  • An electromagnet is magnetic only while current flows.
  • Turning off the power source makes the magnetic field disappear.
  • Reversing the current reverses the magnetic field and swaps the poles.
8) Increasing electromagnet strength
  • Increase the current through the solenoid.
  • Increase the number of turns or pack the turns closer together.
  • Add an iron core, which becomes an induced magnet and greatly strengthens the field.
