Weight & Gravitational Potential Energy

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Gravity & Gravitational Field
  • Gravity = force of attraction between objects.
  • Strength depends on mass of objects and distance apart.
  • Large nearby objects (Earth, Moon) create strong gravitational effects.
  • Each massive body has a gravitational field; its strength is denoted g.
  • Earth: g ≈ 9.8 N/kg; Moon: g ≈ 1.6 N/kg.
2) Weight vs. Mass
  • Mass (m): intrinsic property, measured in kilograms (kg).
  • Weight (W): force due to gravity on mass; W = m × g.
  • Unit of weight = newton (N).
  • Example: 60 kg person on Earth → W = 60 × 9.8 = 588 N.
  • Everyday speech often confuses mass (kg) and weight (N).
3) Work Against Gravity & Gravitational Potential Energy
  • To lift an object, work is done against gravity.
  • Energy transferred → object's gravitational potential energy (GPE) store.
  • Formula: Ep = m × g × h
  • Units: kg × (N/kg) × m = joules (J).
4) Example Calculation
  • Apple mass = 100 g = 0.1 kg.
  • Height = 3 m, g = 9.8 N/kg.
  • GPE = 0.1 × 9.8 × 3 = 2.94 J.
5) Recap
  • Gravity depends on mass and distance.
  • Weight = m × g.
  • Earth’s g ≈ 9.8 N/kg.
  • Gravitational potential energy: Ep = m g h (in joules).
