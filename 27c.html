Density — Formula, Units, and Measuring Density

Notes (rewritten in English from the given speech)

What density means
  • Density describes how much mass is packed into a given volume.
  • It can be calculated using the formula density = mass ÷ volume.
  • The symbol for density is the Greek letter rho (ρ).
Density = Mass / VolumeSymbol: ρPhysical property
Units of density
  • In physics, mass is usually measured in kilograms (kg) and volume in metres cubed (m³).
  • So density is commonly measured in kilograms per cubic metre (kg/m³).
  • Another common unit is grams per cubic centimetre (g/cm³).
  • 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³.
Aluminium example
  • Solid aluminium has a density of 2710 kg/m³.
  • This means a 1 m³ block of aluminium would have a mass of 2710 kg.
  • The same density can also be written as 2.71 g/cm³.
Worked calculation
  • To find volume, rearrange the formula to volume = mass ÷ density.
  • For 420 kg of aluminium with a density of 2710 kg/m³:
  • 420 ÷ 2710 = 0.155
  • So the volume is 0.155 m³.
Finding the density of a solid experimentally
  • To calculate density, you must measure both the mass and the volume of the solid.
  • The mass is measured by placing the object on a balance.
  • For a regular solid, measure its dimensions and calculate the volume using the shape formula.
  • For a cuboid, volume = length × width × height.
  • Example: a cuboid with dimensions 4 cm × 3 cm × 2 cm has a volume of 24 cm³.
Finding the volume of an irregular solid
  • An irregular solid can be measured using a Eureka can filled with water and an empty measuring cylinder.
  • The can has a side outlet, so when the solid is placed into the water, some water flows out.
  • The volume of water collected is equal to the volume of the solid.
  • Once the mass and volume are known, divide mass by volume to find the density.
Finding the density of a liquid
  • Place an empty measuring cylinder on a balance and zero the balance first.
  • Pour a known volume of liquid into the cylinder, for example 10 ml, which is the same as 10 cm³.
  • Record the mass of that liquid.
  • Then calculate density using density = mass ÷ volume.
How to improve accuracy
  • Using a larger volume usually gives a more accurate result because measurement uncertainty has less effect.
  • Taking multiple readings helps identify anomalies.
  • You can then calculate a mean value for better reliability.
