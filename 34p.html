Notes (Key points rewritten from the speech)

Isotopes and Radioactivity
  • Elements exist as isotopes with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons.
  • Only some isotopes are stable; others are unstable and undergo radioactive decay.
  • Radioactive materials contain unstable isotopes that emit radiation to become more stable.
Alpha Radiation
  • Alpha particles consist of 2 protons and 2 neutrons (same as a helium nucleus).
  • They have a +2 charge and relatively large mass.
  • They are highly ionizing but weakly penetrating.
  • Stopped by paper or a few cm of air.
Beta Radiation
  • Beta particles are electrons with a -1 charge.
  • Formed when a neutron decays into a proton and electron.
  • They are moderately ionizing and moderately penetrating.
  • Stopped by thin aluminium (~5 mm).
Gamma Radiation
  • Gamma rays are electromagnetic waves (no mass, no charge).
  • Emitted to release excess energy after other decay.
  • Weakly ionizing but very penetrating.
  • Require thick lead or concrete to stop.
Neutron Emission
  • Occurs when a nucleus has too many neutrons.
  • A neutron is emitted to increase stability.
