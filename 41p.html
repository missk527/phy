41P. Scalars and Vectors

Key Notes (Synced with Video)

0:00 - 0:11 | Scalars and Vectors
  • This video explains the difference between scalar quantities and vector quantities.
  • Scalar quantities may also be called scalars.
  • Vector quantities may also be called vectors.
0:11 - 0:40 | Scalar Quantities
  • Scalars are physical quantities that have magnitude only.
  • Magnitude means size, so it can be measured using a number and a unit.
  • For example, if a car travels at 22 m/s, 22 m/s is the magnitude of its speed.
  • Speed alone has no direction, so it is a scalar quantity.
0:40 - 0:59 | Examples of Scalars and Vectors
  • Examples of scalar quantities include distance, mass, temperature, and time.
  • Vectors have both magnitude and direction.
  • Examples of vector quantities include velocity, displacement, acceleration, force, and momentum.
1:18 - 1:52 | Distance Compared with Displacement
  • If you walk 3 km from point A, distance only tells us how far you travelled.
  • Because distance gives no direction, you could end up anywhere 3 km from the starting point.
  • If you walk 3 km east from point A, your final position is clear.
  • Displacement is a vector because it includes both a magnitude of 3 km and a direction of east.
2:04 - 2:22 | Representing Vectors with Arrows
  • Vectors are represented using arrows.
  • The length of the arrow shows the magnitude of the vector.
  • The direction the arrow points shows the direction of the vector.
  • For example, 4 km north is shown by an arrow pointing north.
2:22 - 2:40 | Comparing and Naming Vectors
  • A 2 km west vector is shown by an arrow pointing left.
  • A 2 km vector is half the length of a 4 km vector.
  • Vectors can also be written as negative vectors.
  • For example, 2 km west can be described as -2 km east because it is opposite to the east direction.
