Physics – Electrical Circuits and Components

  1. Circuit symbols are used to represent components clearly and consistently.
  2. A circuit diagram shows what each component does and the symbol for it.
  3. A power source can be a cell or a battery.
  4. A battery is made of multiple cells.
  5. A switch controls whether current can flow (on or off).
  6. When a switch is closed, the circuit is complete and current flows.
  7. When a switch is open, the circuit is broken and current stops.
  8. This happens because the circuit is no longer complete.
  9. A lamp is represented by a small bulb symbol.
  10. A fuse melts and breaks the circuit when too much current flows.
  11. Fuses prevent damage by stopping excessive current.
  12. A diode allows current to flow in one direction only.
  13. This direction is one-way.
  14. LED stands for Light Emitting Diode.
  15. An LED lights up when current flows in the forward direction.
  16. LEDs are used in alarm clocks, traffic lights, and LED bulbs.
  17. Current is measured using an ammeter.
  18. An ammeter is connected in series.
  19. Potential difference (voltage) is measured using a voltmeter.
  20. A voltmeter is connected in parallel.
  21. Resistance is measured in ohms (Ω).
  22. A resistor limits or reduces current by adding resistance.
  23. A fixed resistor has a set resistance value.
  24. A variable resistor allows resistance to be adjusted easily.
  25. LDR stands for Light Dependent Resistor.
  26. The resistance of an LDR depends on light intensity.
  27. In bright light, the resistance of an LDR is low.
  28. In the dark, the resistance of an LDR is high.
  29. LDRs are used in automatic night lights and burglar alarms.
  30. Light reduces resistance, allowing more current to power the alarm.
  31. A thermistor is a temperature-dependent resistor.
  32. The resistance of a thermistor depends on temperature.
  33. As temperature increases, resistance falls.
  34. As temperature decreases, resistance rises.
  35. Thermistors are used in car engines and electronic thermostats.
  36. They are used to sense and monitor engine temperature.
  37. They also sense temperature for control and regulation.
  38. An ammeter measures current.
  39. A voltmeter measures potential difference.
  40. LDRs and thermistors are examples of sensors.
