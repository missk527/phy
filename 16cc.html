Electric Charge: Q = I × t

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) What is electric charge?
  • This video introduces electric charge and the main equation used to calculate it.
  • Charge is a measure of the total current that has flowed during a certain time.
Definition of charge • Charge (Q) measures how much electric charge has passed a point in a circuit over a certain time. • It depends on: - how big the current is - how long the current flows
  • A water analogy can help:
    • Current is like the rate at which water flows down a river.
    • Charge is like the total amount of water that has flowed past in a minute or an hour.
2) Symbols and units
  • Charge is represented by the letter Q.
  • The unit of charge is the coulomb, written as C.
  • Current is represented by I and is measured in amperes (amps, A).
  • Time is represented by t and is measured in seconds (s).
Key symbols • Q = charge, in coulombs (C) • I = current, in amperes (A) • t = time, in seconds (s)
3) The charge equation: Q = I × t
  • The equation to remember is:
Charge equation Q = I × t Q = charge (C) I = current (A) t = time (s)
  • This means: the total charge is equal to the current multiplied by the time.
  • If the current is bigger, or the current flows for longer, then more charge passes through the circuit.
4) Example 1: kettle question
  • Example: A kettle draws a current of 12 A and takes 50 s to boil.
  • Question: How much charge passed through the wire?
Given: • I = 12 A • t = 50 s Using Q = I × t: Q = 12 × 50 = 600 C So 600 coulombs of charge pass through the wire.
  • In this example the current is already in amps and the time is already in seconds, so we can use the equation directly.
5) Rearranging the formula to find current
  • Sometimes we know the total charge and the time, and we need to find the current.
  • Starting from Q = I × t, we can rearrange the formula by dividing both sides by t.
Rearranged for current Q = I × t → I = Q / t • To find I, divide the total charge by the time.
6) Example 2: phone charger question
  • Example: A phone charger transfers a total charge of 43.2 kC over a period of 2 hours.
  • Question: What current flowed through the wire?
Step 1 – Use I = Q / t Step 2 – Convert units • Charge: 43.2 kC = 43,200 C • Time: 2 hours - 2 × 60 = 120 minutes - 120 × 60 = 7,200 seconds Step 3 – Calculate current I = Q / t = 43,200 C / 7,200 s = 6 A So the current through the charger is 6 A.
  • Always make sure charge is in coulombs and time is in seconds before using the formula.
