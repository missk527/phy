80P. The Motor Effect

Key Notes (Synced with Video)

0:00 - 0:59 | Introduction to the Motor Effect
  • The motor effect happens when a current-carrying wire is placed in a magnetic field.
  • The wire experiences a force because magnetic fields interact.
  • A current-carrying wire produces its own magnetic field.
  • The strongest force occurs when the wire is at 90° to the magnetic field.
  • No force acts when the wire is parallel to the field.
1:06 - 1:49 | Fleming’s Left Hand Rule
  • Fleming’s left hand rule predicts the force direction.
  • The first finger points in the magnetic field direction.
  • The second finger points in the current direction.
  • The thumb points in the force direction.
  • The magnetic field goes from north to south.
2:00 - 3:00 | Horseshoe Magnet Example
  • A current flows through metal rails between magnet poles.
  • The rails create their own magnetic field.
  • The magnetic fields interact and produce a force.
  • Using Fleming’s left hand rule shows the force direction.
  • In the example, the force acts to the right.
3:00 - 4:07 | Calculating Force
  • The force equation is F = BIL.
  • F is force measured in Newtons.
  • B is magnetic flux density measured in Teslas.
  • I is current measured in amps.
  • L is wire length measured in meters.
4:07 - 4:23 | Example Calculation
  • A 10 cm wire carries a 5 A current.
  • The magnetic flux density is 0.4 T.
  • 10 cm must be converted to 0.1 m.
  • Using F = BIL gives 0.2 N.
