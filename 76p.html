Magnetic Fields

Notes

  • A magnet is any object that produces a magnetic field.
  • Magnets come in different shapes such as bar and horseshoe magnets.
  • The Earth itself acts like a giant magnet.
  • All magnets have two poles: north and south.
  • Magnetic fields surround magnets.
  • Field lines are used to represent magnetic fields.
  • Field lines are drawn from the north pole to the south pole.
  • Arrows on field lines show the direction of the magnetic field.
  • Field lines leave the north pole and enter the south pole.
  • Straight and curved lines are used to represent the field around a magnet.
  • Some field lines loop around the magnet.
  • The density of field lines shows field strength.
  • Closer lines mean a stronger magnetic field.
  • The magnetic field is strongest near the poles.
  • We cannot see magnetic field lines directly.
  • A compass can be used to detect magnetic fields.
  • A compass needle is a small magnet.
  • The compass aligns with the magnetic field direction.
  • The compass needle points towards the south pole of a magnet.
  • By moving a compass, we can map field lines.
  • Joining the arrows creates a field line diagram.
  • Like poles repel each other.
  • Unlike poles attract each other.
  • Repulsion happens when two north or two south poles meet.
  • Attraction happens between north and south poles.
  • Field lines explain magnetic forces between magnets.
