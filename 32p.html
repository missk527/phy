History of the Atom

Notes

  • Atomic theory suggests that all matter is made of tiny particles called atoms, separated by empty space.
  • Democritus (around 500 BC) first proposed that matter is made of indivisible particles.
  • John Dalton (1800s) described atoms as solid spheres and suggested different elements have different types of atoms.
  • J.J. Thomson (1897) discovered electrons and proposed the plum pudding model with electrons embedded in a positive sphere.
  • Ernest Rutherford (1909) conducted the gold foil experiment and discovered a small, dense, positively charged nucleus.
  • Rutherford proposed the nuclear model with electrons surrounding the nucleus.
  • Rutherford’s model had a flaw because electrons should collapse into the nucleus.
  • Niels Bohr (1913) suggested electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed shells, preventing collapse.
  • Later experiments showed the nucleus contains protons (positive particles).
  • James Chadwick discovered neutrons, neutral particles in the nucleus.
  • The modern atomic model includes protons and neutrons in the nucleus and electrons in shells.
