Notes (Key points from the speech)

Definition & units
  • Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity (how fast speed increases or decreases).
  • Symbol: a; typical units: m s⁻² (m/s²).
  • It is a vector (has magnitude and direction). Negative acceleration indicates slowing down (deceleration) if direction is unchanged.
Core equations
  • Time form: a = Δv / t, where Δv = v − u (final minus initial velocity).
  • Distance form (SUVAT): v² − u² = 2 a s → rearrange to find distance: s = (v² − u²) / (2a).
  • Choose the equation based on the given quantities: use Δv/t if you have time; use v²−u²=2as if you have distance and velocities.
Worked examples (from the speech)
  • Car: 5 s to go from 15 to 35 m/s → Δv = 20 m/s → a = 20/5 = 4 m/s² (average over 5 s).
  • Ball drop (ignore air resistance): u = 0, v = 7 m/s, a = g = 9.8 m/s² downward → s = (7² − 0)/ (2×9.8) = 49/19.6 = 2.5 m.
Average vs constant acceleration
  • Average acceleration uses overall Δv and total time (real cars may not accelerate uniformly).
  • Uniform/constant acceleration means the same a throughout the motion.
Exam tips
  • If an object starts from rest, then u = 0.
  • Keep units consistent (m/s, s, m).
  • Specify direction/signs for vector quantities when required.
