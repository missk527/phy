Refraction of Light, Ray Diagrams & Prisms

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

What refraction is
  • Refraction happens when a light wave changes direction as it passes from one medium to another (for example, from air into glass).
  • The direction only changes if the ray hits the boundary at an angle. If it enters perpendicular to the surface, it continues straight.
Why light bends: speed changes in different materials
  • Waves travel at different speeds in different materials because materials have different densities.
  • For light (an electromagnetic wave), a more optically dense material makes the wave travel slower.
  • So when light goes from a less dense medium to a more dense medium (air → glass), it slows down.
Bending towards or away from the normal
  • The normal is an imaginary line drawn perpendicular to the surface at the point where the ray hits.
  • Entering a more dense medium (air → glass): the ray bends towards the normal.
  • Entering a less dense medium (glass → air): the ray bends away from the normal.
How to draw a refraction ray diagram through a glass block
  1. Draw the incident ray and mark the point of incidence where it hits the surface.
  2. Draw the normal at 90° to the surface at that point.
  3. Inside the glass, draw the refracted ray bending towards the normal (because the light slows down in glass).
  4. Continue the refracted ray to the far side of the glass.
  5. At the second surface, draw another normal.
  6. Draw the emergent ray bending away from the normal as it leaves the glass into air.

Tip: In exams, the exact bend is usually not judged precisely—what matters is the correct direction (towards/away from the normal).

Angles to label
  • Angle of incidence (i): between the incident ray and the normal.
  • Angle of refraction (r): between the refracted ray and the normal (in the second medium).
Frequency vs wavelength when speed changes
  • Wave speed depends on frequency and wavelength: v = f × λ.
  • When light enters a new medium, its frequency stays the same.
  • So a change in speed means the wavelength changes:
    • If speed decreases, wavelength decreases.
    • If speed increases, wavelength increases.
Triangular prisms and dispersion
  • Different wavelengths of visible light refract by different amounts.
  • White light contains many wavelengths, so in a triangular prism each colour bends a bit differently.
  • This spreads the light into a spectrum (a rainbow effect), called dispersion.
