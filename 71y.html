Notes

  • Visible light is a spectrum of colors from red (long wavelength, low frequency) to violet (short wavelength, high frequency).
  • The order of colors can be remembered as ROYGBIV.
  • White light contains all wavelengths, while black is the absence of light.
  • The color of an object depends on the light hitting it and its properties.
  • Objects appear colored based on which wavelengths are absorbed, reflected, or transmitted.
  • Opaque objects do not transmit light; they reflect or absorb it.
  • The color of opaque objects is determined by reflected wavelengths.
  • Objects may reflect combinations of wavelengths to produce colors (e.g. red + green = yellow).
  • Transparent objects transmit almost all light, making them see-through.
  • Translucent objects transmit some light, partially allowing vision through them.
  • The color of translucent objects depends on transmitted wavelengths.
  • Color filters allow only certain wavelengths to pass through.
  • Primary color filters transmit red, green, or blue light only.
  • A green filter transmits green light and absorbs other wavelengths.
  • Objects appear black through a filter if their reflected light is blocked.
  • Secondary color filters (e.g. yellow) transmit combinations of primary colors.
