Ray Diagrams — Convex and Concave Lenses

Notes (rewritten in English from the given speech)

Concave lenses (diverging lenses)
  • A concave lens is a diverging lens, so it makes light rays spread out.
  • In ray diagrams, a concave lens is shown wider at the ends and thinner in the middle.
  • The diagram includes a horizontal principal axis, a focal point (F) on each side, and often points labelled 2F.
Drawing rays for a concave lens
  • Choose a point at the top of the object.
  • Draw one incident ray parallel to the axis.
  • Draw a second ray through the centre of the lens; this ray continues straight because it is not refracted.
  • The parallel ray is refracted so that it appears to come from the focal point on the same side of the lens.
  • Use a dashed virtual ray back to the focal point to show this construction.
Image formed by a concave lens
  • The image is virtual because it is found by extending rays backward.
  • The image is upright.
  • The image is smaller than the object.
  • A concave lens always produces a virtual, upright, diminished image.
Convex lenses (converging lenses)
  • A convex lens is a converging lens, so it brings light rays together.
  • In diagrams, a convex lens is thicker in the middle and narrower at the ends.
  • It is drawn with the same axis, focal points, and 2F markings.
Drawing rays for a convex lens
  • Start from the top of the object.
  • A ray through the centre of the lens continues straight.
  • A ray parallel to the axis is refracted through the focal point on the opposite side.
  • The image is located where the refracted rays actually meet.
Image rules for convex lenses
  • If the object is beyond 2F, the image is real, inverted, and smaller.
  • If the object is at 2F, the image is real, inverted, and the same size.
  • If the object is between F and 2F, the image is real, inverted, and larger.
  • If the object is between F and the lens, the refracted rays do not meet, so backward virtual rays are used. The image is then virtual, upright, and larger.
Useful exam reminders
  • In exams, the object is usually a simple arrow, not a detailed picture.
  • Always draw rays from the top of the arrow.
  • Be ready to describe images as real or virtual, upright or inverted, and larger, smaller, or the same size.
