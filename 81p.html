81P. Electric Motors

Key Notes (Synced with Video)

0:00 - 0:33 | Motor Effect
  • A current-carrying wire placed in a magnetic field experiences a force.
  • This effect is called the motor effect.
  • The direction of the force depends on the direction of the current and the magnetic field.
  • Fleming’s left-hand rule is used to work out the direction of the force.
0:33 - 1:16 | Forces on Opposite Sides of a Coil
  • If current flows out of the page and the magnetic field points right, the wire experiences an upward force.
  • If the current flows in the opposite direction, the force also acts in the opposite direction.
  • A coil has current flowing around both sides of the wire loop.
  • The two sides of the coil experience forces in opposite directions.
1:16 - 2:21 | Why the Coil Needs Help to Keep Spinning
  • One side of the coil is pushed upward while the other side is pushed downward, so the coil starts to rotate.
  • After half a turn, the current direction in each side of the coil reverses.
  • The forces also reverse, so the coil would turn back the other way.
  • Without a special device, the coil would only flip back and forth instead of rotating continuously.
2:21 - 3:04 | Split-Ring Commutator
  • To keep the coil rotating in the same direction, the current must reverse every half turn.
  • A split-ring commutator swaps the positive and negative connections every half turn.
  • This reverses the current at the correct moment.
  • As a result, the forces keep producing rotation in the same direction.
3:11 - 4:06 | Current Flow in a Motor
  • The positive terminal sends current into one side of the commutator.
  • The current flows around the coil and leaves through the negative terminal.
  • The coil experiences clockwise forces and starts to turn.
  • The commutator rotates with the coil while the terminals stay fixed.
  • Electric motors convert electrical energy into rotational motion.
4:12 - 4:31 | Increasing Motor Speed
  • Real motors often need to be more powerful than a simple demonstration motor.
  • The rotation speed can be increased by increasing the current in the wire.
  • Adding more turns to the coil also increases the turning effect.
  • Using stronger magnets increases the magnetic flux density and makes the motor rotate faster.
