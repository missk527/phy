Geothermal Energy: How it Works, Pros & Cons

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

What is geothermal energy?
  • Geothermal energy is heat stored within the Earth’s crust.
  • Heat sources:
    • Residual heat from Earth’s formation.
    • Ongoing radioactive decay of materials (e.g., uranium) deep underground.
  • It’s the same energy that warms natural hot springs.
Two main uses
  • Direct heating (cheaper): pump water down to warm it, then pump it back to heat buildings.
  • Electricity generation: keep water underground until it becomes steam. Rising steam spins turbines that drive generators.
Where it works best
  • Best in areas with hot rocks near the surface, e.g., volcanic regions.
  • Power plants can be expensive to build.
Pros & cons (from the speech)
  • Pros:
    • Low environmental damage when installing.
    • While running, produces no pollutants like CO₂.
    • Renewable resource.
    • Reliable over long periods.
  • Cons:
    • Only usable in certain locations.
    • Plants can be costly to build.
