1) What is kinetic energy?

Kinetic energy (KE) is the energy an object has because it is moving. Any moving thing ‚Äî from a plane to a tiny particle ‚Äî stores energy in its kinetic energy store.

2) What affects kinetic energy?
  • Speed: Faster motion ‚Üí more KE, because more energy must be transferred to speed the object up.
  • Mass: For the same speed, a more massive object has more KE.
3) The equation
Kinetic energy:   Ek = ¬Ω m v2

  • m in kilograms (kg)
  • v in metres per second (m/s)
4) Units & conversions (watch out!)
  • 1 tonne = 1000 kg (so 20 tonnes = 20,000 kg).
  • 1 g = 0.001 kg (so 0.1 g = 0.0001 kg).
  • Energy in kJ: divide joules by 1000 (e.g., 250,000 J = 250 kJ).
