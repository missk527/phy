74. Gas Pressure and Particle Behavior

Key Notes (English)

Adapted from the speech: gas particles and pressure.

1) Particle Motion
  • Gas particles move randomly in all directions.
  • They collide with container walls and rebound.
2) What Creates Pressure
  • Collisions of particles with walls exert force.
  • Pressure = force ÷ area.
3) Factors Affecting Pressure
  • Pressure depends on number of collisions and force of each collision.
4) Temperature Effect
  • Higher temperature increases kinetic energy.
  • Particles move faster → more collisions → greater force.
  • Pressure increases.
5) Concentration Effect
  • More particles in same volume → more collisions.
  • Pressure increases.
6) Volume Effect
  • Smaller volume → particles closer → more collisions.
  • Pressure increases as volume decreases.
7) Flexible Containers
  • Containers like balloons can expand.
  • Changes may increase volume instead of pressure.
  • In reality, both pressure and volume may increase.
