Notes (Key points from the speech)

Types of lenses and refraction
  • Convex (converging) lens: bulges outward and refracts parallel rays inwards to meet at the principal focus.
  • Concave (diverging) lens: curves inward and refracts parallel rays outwards, spreading (dispersing) the light.
Principal focus, axis, and focal length
  • Every lens has a principal focus on both sides, the same distance from the centre (lenses are symmetrical).
  • The principal axis is the straight line through the centre of the lens; the principal focus lies on this axis.
  • Focal length = distance from the lens centre to the principal focus.
Lens power and curvature
  • A shorter focal length means a more powerful lens (it bends light more strongly).
  • To increase power: make the lens more curved or use a material with a higher refractive index.
Real vs virtual images
  • Images form where rays from a point actually meet (real) or appear to meet (virtual).
  • Real images: rays really converge; the image can be captured on a screen (e.g., the eye forms a real image on the retina, inverted).
  • Virtual images: rays do not truly converge; we trace virtual rays backwards (e.g., a mirror image appears behind the mirror).
Describing images in exams
  • State whether the image is real or virtual.
  • Also describe orientation (upright or inverted) and size (magnified or diminished).
