Conservation of Energy

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) The Principle
  • Conservation of energy: energy can be transferred, stored, or dissipated, but can never be created or destroyed.
  • Energy only moves between objects and between forms (e.g., electrical → chemical → light).
TransferStoreDissipateCannot be created/destroyed
2) Phone charging + using (energy pathways)
  • Charging: electrical energy from the mains travels along the wire and is transferred to the battery’s chemical energy store.
  • Using the phone: chemical energy → electrical energy which flows around the internal circuit and powers components.
  • At the screen: electrical → light (so you can see). At the speaker: electrical → sound.
3) Useful vs wasted (dissipated) energy
  • Ideally, all transferred energy would go into the desired store.
  • In reality, some energy is dissipated to other forms (i.e., wasted relative to our goal).
  • Commonly dissipated as heat (phone warms when charging or heavy use) but can also be sound, etc.
4) Systems: open vs closed
  • A system is the portion of the physical universe you choose to analyze; everything else is the environment.
  • Open system: energy and matter can move between system and environment (e.g., the phone by itself).
  • Closed system: energy and matter cannot enter or escape (e.g., the sealed jar containing the phone and the air).
  • In the jar example, heat from the phone is dissipated to the air inside the jar, so it stays within the system.
  • Total energy of a closed system does not change.
