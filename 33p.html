Atomic Structure

Notes

  • An atom has a nucleus containing protons and neutrons.
  • Protons have a +1 charge and neutrons have no charge.
  • Both protons and neutrons have a relative mass of 1.
  • Electrons orbit the nucleus in shells and have a -1 charge.
  • Electrons are much smaller (about 1/2000 the mass of protons).
  • Atomic number = number of protons (defines the element).
  • Mass number = protons + neutrons.
  • Number of electrons = number of protons in a neutral atom.
  • Isotopes have the same number of protons but different neutrons.
  • This gives isotopes different mass numbers.
  • Some isotopes are stable, others are unstable.
  • Unstable isotopes undergo radioactive decay.
  • They emit alpha, beta, or gamma radiation.
  • Electrons exist in energy levels (shells).
  • Higher shells have more energy and are further from the nucleus.
  • Electrons can gain energy and move to higher shells (excited state).
  • This energy comes from electromagnetic radiation.
  • Electrons fall back down and release energy as radiation.
  • Ionisation happens when an electron gains enough energy to leave the atom.
  • This forms a positive ion.
  • Ionising radiation removes electrons from atoms.
