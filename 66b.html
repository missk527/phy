Microwaves & Infrared — Uses and Hazards Satellites · Heating · IR

Notes (Key points from the speech)

Position in the electromagnetic spectrum
  • Microwaves and infrared (IR) are the 2nd and 3rd groups in the electromagnetic spectrum (from the long-wavelength end).
  • Because they are on the left side of the spectrum, they have relatively long wavelengths and low frequencies.
Microwaves for satellite communication
  • Some microwaves are not absorbed by water molecules.
  • These are used for satellite communication because the waves must pass through the Earth’s atmosphere, which contains water.
  • Microwaves are sent up to a satellite, then the satellite transmits them back to Earth.
  • We detect the signal using a satellite dish (e.g., for satellite TV).
Microwaves in ovens (heating food)
  • Other microwaves are absorbed by water molecules.
  • In a microwave oven, water molecules absorb microwave energy and vibrate more.
  • Vibrating water molecules transfer energy to nearby particles so heat spreads through the food by conduction and/or convection.
  • Foods with more water usually heat up faster.
Infrared radiation and temperature
  • Infrared radiation is emitted by all objects that have thermal (heat) energy.
  • The hotter the object, the more infrared it emits.
Infrared cameras (thermal imaging)
  • Infrared cameras detect IR to show which areas are hot and which are cold.
  • This helps us see in the dark and spot living organisms.
  • Warm animals appear bright because they emit lots of IR; cooler surroundings appear darker.
Infrared for cooking
  • Hot metal in ovens and grills emits lots of infrared radiation, which transfers heat to food and cooks it.
  • IR does not penetrate deeply into food, so heating is mainly at the surface.
  • That’s why bread toasts in a toaster instead of warming evenly like in a microwave.
Infrared heaters
  • Electric heaters use electrical energy to heat metal elements.
  • The hot element emits infrared radiation that warms the surroundings (heats rooms).
Hazards
  • Microwaves and infrared are generally safe at background levels.
  • They can be harmful in high quantities because they transfer large amounts of energy.
  • Microwaves can heat living tissue (cells can boil and be destroyed).
  • Strong infrared from grills/toasters can cause burns by damaging skin cells.
Take-home message
  • Microwaves can be used for communication (if not absorbed by water) and for heating (if absorbed by water).
  • Infrared is emitted by warm objects and is useful for thermal imaging, cooking, and heating rooms.
  • Both are mainly harmful only at high intensities due to heating effects.
