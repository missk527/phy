Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Pressure in gases
  • Gas pressure is caused by particles colliding with the walls of a container.
  • More collisions or stronger collisions lead to higher pressure.
2) Factors affecting pressure
  • Pressure increases if temperature increases.
  • Pressure increases if the number of particles increases.
  • Pressure increases if the volume decreases.
3) Pressure and volume relationship
  • Pressure and volume are inversely proportional.
  • If volume increases, pressure decreases, and vice versa.
4) Boyle’s Law
  • For a fixed amount of gas at constant temperature:
  • Pressure × Volume = constant
  • This can also be written as P₁V₁ = P₂V₂.
5) Example calculation 1
  • Initial: P₁ = 100 Pa, V₁ = 1.5 m³
  • Final: V₂ = 0.3 m³
  • P₁V₁ = 150 → P₂ × 0.3 = 150
  • P₂ = 500 Pa
6) Example calculation 2
  • Initial: P₁ = 101 kPa, V₁ = 1800 L
  • Final: V₂ = 12 L
  • P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
  • P₂ = (101 × 1800) ÷ 12 = 15150 kPa
